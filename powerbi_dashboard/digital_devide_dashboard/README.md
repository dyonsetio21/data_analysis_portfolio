# Understanding the Global Digital Divide

## Project Overview
This project analyzes global internet adoption trends and explores how economic prosperity, education, and population relate to internet usage across countries from 2010 to 2023.

## Objectives
- Analyze global internet adoption trends
- Identify countries with the highest and lowest internet usage
- Explore the relationship between GDP per capita and internet adoption
- Explore the relationship between education and internet adoption
- Highlight the existence of the global digital divide

## Dataset Sources
- [Our World in Data (Internet Usage)](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/share-of-individuals-using-the-internet.csv)
- [World Bank (GDP per-Capita)](https://api.worldbank.org/v2/en/indicator/NY.GDP.PCAP.CD?downloadformat=csv) 
- [World Bank (Education)](https://api.worldbank.org/v2/en/indicator/SE.SEC.ENRR?downloadformat=csv)
- [World Bank (Population)](https://api.worldbank.org/v2/en/indicator/SP.POP.TOTL?downloadformat=csv)

## Key Findings
- Global internet adoption increased from 34.1% in 2010 to 72.0% in 2023.
- High-income countries dominate the highest internet penetration rankings.
- GDP per capita is positively associated with internet usage.
- Education levels are also associated with higher internet adoption.
- A significant digital divide remains between developed and developing countries.
