# Rossmann Sales Perorrmance Dashboard - Retail Sales Patterns

## Project Overview
This project analyzes sales data from Rossmann, a retail store chain, to understand what drives sales performance. The data was explored using Microsoft Excel and visualized through an interactive dashboard in Microsoft Power BI.

The main questions this project tried to answer:
- Do promotions actually help increase sales?
- Which store type performs the best?
- What days of the week have the highest sales?
- Are there seasonal sales patterns?
- Does school holiday affect sales?

## Insight
1. **Promotions work really well** — Sales go up by about 38% on days when promotions are running, compared to days without promotions.
2. **Store Type B leads in sales** — Among all store types, Type B has the highest average daily sales. This suggests it has advantages in location, operations, or sales strategy.
3. **Sales vary by day of the week** — Sunday and Monday are the busiest days, while Saturday is the slowest day for sales.
4. **School holidays give a small boost** — Sales increase around 4.4% during school holiday periods, showing that more customers are active during those times.
5. **Sales peak at the end of the year** — There is a clear spike in sales in December, likely driven by the holiday season and increased shopping activity.

## Recomenndations
1. **Run promotions on slow days** — Focus promotions on Saturdays to lift sales on the weakest day of the week.
2. **Learn from top-performing stores** — Study what makes Store Type B successful and apply those strategies to other store types.
3. **Prepare well for busy days** — Make sure enough staff and stock are ready on Sundays to handle the high number of customers.
4. **Take advantage of seasonal moments** — Use targeted promotions during year-end and school holiday periods to maximize sales potential.
